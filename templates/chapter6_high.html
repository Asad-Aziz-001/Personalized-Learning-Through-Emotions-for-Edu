1. Circles

Circle Equations and Properties

A circle is the set of all points equidistant from a fixed point called the center. Understanding circle equations helps us solve geometric problems.

Standard Forms of Circle Equations:
‚Ä¢ Center at origin: \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\)
‚Ä¢ Center at (h,k): \((x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2\)
‚Ä¢ General form: \(x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0\)
üéØ Every point on a circle is equally distant from the center!

Point
Example: Find center and radius of \(x^2 + y^2 + 12x - 10y = 0\)
Step 1: Group x and y terms: \((x^2 + 12x) + (y^2 - 10y) = 0\)
Step 2: Complete the square for x: \(x^2 + 12x = (x + 6)^2 - 36\)
Step 3: Complete the square for y: \(y^2 - 10y = (y - 5)^2 - 25\)
Step 4: Substitute back: \((x + 6)^2 - 36 + (y - 5)^2 - 25 = 0\)
Step 5: Simplify: \((x + 6)^2 + (y - 5)^2 = 61\)
Step 6: Center = (-6, 5), Radius = ‚àö61
Example: Find circle through points A(4,5), B(-4,-3), C(8,-3)
Step 1: General form: \(x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0\)
Step 2: For A(4,5): \(16 + 25 + 8g + 10f + c = 0\) ‚Üí \(8g + 10f + c = -41\)
Step 3: For B(-4,-3): \(16 + 9 - 8g - 6f + c = 0\) ‚Üí \(-8g - 6f + c = -25\)
Step 4: For C(8,-3): \(64 + 9 + 16g - 6f + c = 0\) ‚Üí \(16g - 6f + c = -73\)
Step 5: Solve system: g = -2, f = 1, c = -35
Step 6: Equation: \(x^2 + y^2 - 4x + 2y - 35 = 0\)
Step 7: Standard form: \((x - 2)^2 + (y + 1)^2 = 40\)

2. Tangents and Normals

Tangents and Normals to Circles

A tangent touches a circle at exactly one point and is perpendicular to the radius at that point. The normal is perpendicular to the tangent and passes through the center.

Key Formulas:
‚Ä¢ Tangent at (x‚ÇÅ,y‚ÇÅ) on \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\): \(xx‚ÇÅ + yy‚ÇÅ = r^2\)
‚Ä¢ Tangent at (x‚ÇÅ,y‚ÇÅ) on general circle: \(xx‚ÇÅ + yy‚ÇÅ + g(x+x‚ÇÅ) + f(y+y‚ÇÅ) + c = 0\)
‚Ä¢ Normal passes through center and point of contact
üìè Tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of contact!
Point of Contact
Example: Tangent and normal to \(x^2 + y^2 = 25\) at (4,3)
Step 1: Verify point lies on circle: \(4^2 + 3^2 = 16 + 9 = 25\) ‚úì
Step 2: Equation of tangent: \(xx‚ÇÅ + yy‚ÇÅ = r^2\) ‚Üí \(4x + 3y = 25\)
Step 3: Slope of tangent: From \(4x + 3y = 25\) ‚Üí \(y = (-4/3)x + 25/3\), slope = -4/3
Step 4: Slope of radius: From center (0,0) to (4,3) ‚Üí slope = 3/4
Step 5: Slope of normal = negative reciprocal of tangent slope = 3/4
Step 6: Equation of normal: \(y - 3 = (3/4)(x - 4)\) ‚Üí \(3x - 4y = 0\)
Example: Show 3x - 2y = 0 is tangent to \(x^2 + y^2 + 6x - 4y = 0\)
Step 1: Find center and radius of circle
Step 2: Complete square: \((x^2 + 6x) + (y^2 - 4y) = 0\)
Step 3: \((x + 3)^2 - 9 + (y - 2)^2 - 4 = 0\) ‚Üí \((x + 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 = 13\)
Step 4: Center = (-3, 2), Radius = ‚àö13
Step 5: Find distance from center to line 3x - 2y = 0
Step 6: Distance = \(|3(-3) - 2(2)| / ‚àö(3^2 + (-2)^2) = |-9 - 4| / ‚àö13 = 13/‚àö13 = ‚àö13\)
Step 7: Since distance = radius, the line is tangent to the circle

3. Ellipses

Ellipse Equations and Properties

An ellipse is the set of all points where the sum of distances to two fixed points (foci) is constant. Understanding ellipse equations helps us solve geometric problems.

Standard Forms of Ellipse Equations:
‚Ä¢ Horizontal major axis: \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\)
‚Ä¢ Vertical major axis: \(\frac{x^2}{b^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2} = 1\)
‚Ä¢ Relationship: \(c^2 = a^2 - b^2\) where c is distance from center to focus
üéØ Sum of distances from any point on ellipse to foci = 2a!

Example: Find center, foci, vertices of \(\frac{x^2}{16} + \frac{y^2}{9} = 1\)
Step 1: Compare with standard form \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\)
Step 2: \(a^2 = 16\) ‚Üí \(a = 4\), \(b^2 = 9\) ‚Üí \(b = 3\)
Step 3: Center = (0, 0)
Step 4: \(c^2 = a^2 - b^2 = 16 - 9 = 7\) ‚Üí \(c = \sqrt{7}\)
Step 5: Foci = (¬±‚àö7, 0)
Step 6: Vertices = (¬±4, 0)
Step 7: Eccentricity \(e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{7}}{4}\)
Example: Write equation with foci (¬±3, 0) and vertices (¬±5, 0)
Step 1: Foci are horizontal, so major axis is horizontal
Step 2: Center is midpoint between foci: (0, 0)
Step 3: Distance from center to focus: c = 3
Step 4: Distance from center to vertex: a = 5
Step 5: Find b using \(c^2 = a^2 - b^2\)
Step 6: \(9 = 25 - b^2\) ‚Üí \(b^2 = 16\) ‚Üí \(b = 4\)
Step 7: Equation: \(\frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1\)

4. Real-World Applications

Solving Practical Problems with Conic Sections

Conic sections have numerous real-world applications in astronomy, engineering, architecture, and more. These problems involve applying circle and ellipse concepts to practical scenarios.

Common Application Areas:
‚Ä¢ Planetary orbits (ellipses)
‚Ä¢ Satellite dishes (parabolas)
‚Ä¢ Architectural arches (ellipses and circles)
‚Ä¢ Telescope mirrors (parabolas)

Real-World Applications
Application: Planetary Orbit
Problem: A planet orbits the sun in an elliptical path with the sun at one focus. Distance from sun ranges from 17 million miles to 183 million miles.
Step 1: Minimum distance = a - c = 17
Step 2: Maximum distance = a + c = 183
Step 3: Add equations: 2a = 200 ‚Üí a = 100
Step 4: Subtract: 2c = 166 ‚Üí c = 83
Step 5: \(b^2 = a^2 - c^2 = 10000 - 6889 = 3111\)
Step 6: Equation: \(\frac{x^2}{10000} + \frac{y^2}{3111} = 1\)
Application: Elliptical Arch
Problem: An elliptical arch is 90m wide at the base and 30m high at the center.
Step 1: Place coordinate system with center at (0,0)
Step 2: Width = 90m ‚Üí semi-major axis a = 45
Step 3: Height = 30m ‚Üí semi-minor axis b = 30
Step 4: Equation: \(\frac{x^2}{2025} + \frac{y^2}{900} = 1\)
Step 5: At what distance from center is the arch 20‚àö2 m high?
Step 6: Substitute y = 20‚àö2 into equation
Step 7: \(\frac{x^2}{2025} + \frac{(20‚àö2)^2}{900} = 1\) ‚Üí \(\frac{x^2}{2025} + \frac{800}{900} = 1\)
Step 8: \(\frac{x^2}{2025} = 1 - \frac{8}{9} = \frac{1}{9}\) ‚Üí \(x^2 = 225\) ‚Üí \(x = ¬±15\)
